Marco Polo's Journey Through Asia

Marco Polo's travels through Asia were some of the most impressive and throrough recorded adventures in this amazing area of the world. When you travel with Big Gregg tours on this journey, you'll see everything that Marco Polo saw those hundreds of years ago. You'll travel part of the route he traveled. He went from the capital of Azerbaijan to the court of Kublai Khan!

Travel Time: 10 Days

Day One: Flight to Tabriz

Beginning: 1275

Tabriz

When Marco Polo visited Tabriz in 1275, it was the Mongol capital of Azarbin (Azerbaijan) under the Ikhanid dynasty. This beautiful area is rich in historical significance. It's peak was under the rule of Ghazan Khan, when great people from all over the world were drawn there. This includes Ibn Battuta, the great Berber traveller, and Khajeh Rashid od-Din Fazlollah, the minister that built the Rob'e Rashidi center.

Eastern Tabriz

Locations

  • Days 2-3: El-Goli Park was originally a summer palace for Iranian royals. Now, it's a hike leading to an artificial lake and a restaurant made form the converted palace.
  • Day 4: Saat Tower was the original city office. It has since been converted into the Municipality Museum, where you can find some of Tabriz's historical artifacts.
  • Day 5-6: Nimeh Shabann is the birthday of Mahdai. There are firework displays all throughout the city. Simply being in the city during this time is an incredible experience.

Badakhshan

Marco Polo went to Badakhshan, and ended up becoming ill. He had to stay there for a year before he recovered and could leave! Despite this, it was still an important city. It was an important trade city, with the excange of things such as lapis lazuli. it was also conveniently stationed along the Silk Road, meaning that it was one of the cities that built the connection and innovation in Africa. Marco Polo wrote that the city had Balbas rubies, which came from the Gorno Badakhshan mines.

Badakhshan Province

Locations

  • Day 7: Flight to Kherqa-ye Sharif: It's an Islamic shrine. It's connected to a mosque, and next to the tomb of the founder of the Durrani Empire, Ahmad Shah Durrani.

Gobi Desert

The Gobi Desert is very large, but still had an established route across it. Marco Polo wrote that it would take a year to go across it, and a month at the narrowest part. It was a large, sandy expanse of nothing but, well, sand! Not much historically happened here, but there are still some cool places worth visiting.

The Gobi Desert was very sandy

Locations

  • Day 8: Flight to the Flaming Cliffs: They are a region of the Gobi Desert where many fossils have been found. Dinosaur eggs were first discovered there.
  • Day 9: Dunhuang is a town that was an epicenter of trade. It was located along the Silk Road, and served as a channel between China and the rest of the world.

End: 1295
